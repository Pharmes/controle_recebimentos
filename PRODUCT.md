# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Multiple internal profiles use this panel across the receiving workflow: branch counter staff, logistics, PCP, and management. They need a shared operational view that makes formula movement, receiving status, and unresolved issues clear without needing to interpret an ERP-style screen.

## Product Purpose

Controle de Recebimento is an internal dashboard for tracking compounded formulas through receiving states: a receber, recebido, and pendentes. It supports daily monitoring of arrivals, receiving confirmation, pending-case identification, and operational export/reporting. Success means teams can understand the current receiving situation quickly, act on exceptions, and share reliable status data across roles.

## Brand Personality

Premium, precise, and calm. The interface should preserve the Pharmes identity from `design.md`: sophisticated pharmacy aesthetics, black/gold/green/cream palette, elegant Cormorant Garamond headings, and modern Montserrat UI text. The premium feeling should come through polish, hierarchy, and restraint rather than decorative excess.

## Anti-references

Avoid looking like a generic ERP, a common SaaS dashboard template, a cold hospital system, an over-luxury boutique interface, or a visually crowded control room. Do not drift away from the existing `design.md` identity, color variables, typography, and component expectations.

## Design Principles

Keep operations first: every visual decision should help users see receiving status, deadlines, exceptions, and next actions faster.

Express premium through restraint: use Pharmes colors and typography with discipline, avoiding decorative clutter or excessive luxury cues.

Make status unmistakable: a receber, recebido, and pendentes must remain visually distinct, scannable, and accessible across desktop and mobile.

Use one consistent component language: buttons, filters, cards, chips, forms, and exports should behave and read as one coherent internal tool.

Design for shared accountability: logistics, branch, PCP, and management should see the same truth without needing role-specific interpretation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target at least WCAG AA contrast for text, status labels, controls, and focus states. Status cannot rely on color alone; labels, icons, and clear copy should support color indicators. Motion should be minimal, state-driven, and respect reduced-motion preferences. The interface should remain usable on narrow screens for operational checks away from a desk.
